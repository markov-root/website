# Semiconductor Industry Evolution

# A Brief History of Chip Manufacturing

Before 2017, most of the world used Deep Ultra Violet (DUV) technology to produce microchips. Intel dominated this industry, with designs for chips multiple generations ahead but only releasing incremental improvements annually. Their market dominance led to complacency.

Intel invested in Extreme Ultra Violet (EUV) technology, developed in collaboration with ASML from the Netherlands around 2016. EUV enabled higher node density on chips, with the key advantage of digital control rather than requiring hardware modifications like DUV. However, Intel's market leadership made them hesitant to adopt this new technology.

While Intel remained complacent, TSMC saw an opportunity. ASML approached TSMC, who eagerly adopted EUV technology and achieved remarkable success. This left Intel struggling to catch up.

By 2023, Intel finally produced their first EUV chips. However, ASML had already developed High Numerical Aperture EUV (H-NA-EUV). Learning from past mistakes, Intel became an early adopter of H-NA-EUV technology, while TSMC, now the industry leader, showed signs of the same complacency that once affected Intel.
